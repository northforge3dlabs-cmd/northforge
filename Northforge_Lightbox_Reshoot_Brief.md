# For use day-of with your new lightbox tent

## Before You Start — Room & Equipment Setup

1. Choose a room you can dim or darken
   Overhead room lighting creates colour casts and competes
   with your lightbox. Close blinds, turn off overhead lights.
   Rely entirely on the lightbox LEDs for illumination.
   This is the single most important setup step.

2. Place lightbox on a stable table at standing height
   Shooting at standing height gives you a natural slight
   downward angle on the product. Avoid shooting upward —
   it distorts the case shape and looks unflattering.

3. Use WHITE backdrop only
   Your lightbox likely came with white, black, and grey sheets.
   Use white for all shots today. Black case against white
   background gives you the maximum contrast you need for
   clean background removal later.

4. Curve the backdrop smoothly
   The backdrop sheet should curve from the back wall of the
   lightbox down to the floor — no visible crease or fold.
   This creates an "infinity" background with no horizon line.
   If it won't lie flat, use a small piece of tape at the
   back top edge to hold the curve.

5. Light positioning
   Most lightboxes have two side panels and optionally a top.
   Turn on ALL available lights for even illumination.
   If your lights are adjustable brightness, set both sides
   to equal intensity. Uneven lighting creates shadows that
   make background removal harder.

6. Mount your phone on the tripod
   Position tripod outside the lightbox opening, roughly
   centred on the opening, at product midpoint height.
   You will adjust angle per shot type — but always work
   from the tripod. Do not hand-hold today.

## Android Camera Settings

Open your native Camera app (not Instagram or any third-party).

Settings to enable before shooting:
- Grid lines: ON (helps keep product centred and straight)
- Timer: set to 2 seconds (eliminates camera shake at shutter)
- Resolution: maximum available on your device
- Scene mode / AI scene: OFF if available
  (auto-enhancement can alter colours unpredictably)

If your Samsung/Android has Pro mode:
- ISO: 100 (lowest available — lightbox gives you plenty of light)
- White balance: set to match your lightbox colour temperature
  (most LED lightboxes are ~5500K — use "Daylight" preset)
  OR set to Auto White Balance and check first test shot
- Shutter speed: let camera auto-select in Pro mode unless
  you see blur, in which case go to 1/100 or faster

Focus technique:
- Tap the product body to set focus point before each shot
- Tap and hold until you see AE/AF LOCK (or equivalent)
  This locks both focus and exposure so it doesn't shift
  between frames
- Take 3 frames of each setup — pick the sharpest one later

## Product Positioning Inside Lightbox

General rules:
- Centre the product left-right within the lightbox
- Leave equal space on left and right sides
- Leave a small gap between the product base and the
  front edge of the lightbox floor (~5-8cm of white showing)
- Product should not touch the backdrop behind it —
  leave ~10cm of space so shadow falls behind, not on, product

For the token tray shots:
- Position tray towards the front of the lightbox floor
- Angle it slightly (10-15°) rather than perfectly straight-on
  — diagonal compositions feel more dynamic

## Shot List

Work through this in order. Complete all shots for one
product before moving to the next. Do not switch back
and forth between products.

---

### PRODUCT 1: SKIRMISH CASE (all colour variants)

Complete all 4 shot types for BLACK first, then repeat
for BLUE, RED, YELLOW. Purple is not a launch colour
so shoot it last or skip if time is short.

#### SHOT TYPE A — Hero Exterior (closed case)
Camera position: 3/4 angle
- Stand tripod to the left of centre, pointing slightly right
- Camera height: slightly above the top of the case
  (roughly level with the handle top)
- Angle: approximately 30-40° to the front face of the case
- This angle shows: front face + top surface + left side
- The accent colour handle and latches should be prominent

What to check before shooting:
- Both latches visible and closed
- Handle upright (not flopped to one side)
- No fingerprints on the case surface
  (wipe with a microfibre cloth before each variant)
- Backdrop curve is smooth behind the case

Frames to take: 3 (pick sharpest in edit)

---

#### SHOT TYPE B — Front Face (closed case)
Camera position: straight-on, level
- Move tripod to directly in front of the lightbox opening
- Camera height: level with the vertical midpoint of the case
- No angle — shoot straight at the front face
- Shows: full front panel, both latches, door pull handle

This shot is used as the colour variant selector image
in Shopify — customers see it when clicking Red/Blue/Yellow.
It must be perfectly straight and centred.

Frames to take: 3

---

#### SHOT TYPE C — Open Case with Miniatures
Camera position: same 3/4 angle as Shot Type A
- Open the front door and lay it flat/down as in your
  existing shots
- Place 3-4 miniatures on the open door tray
  (more than 1 mini — shows scale better)
- Interior rail system should be clearly visible
- Accent colour should be visible on handle, door, rails

Styling:
- Arrange minis naturally — not in a perfect row
- One mini can be slightly in front of others for depth
- Make sure minis are painted (not bare plastic) if possible

Frames to take: 3

---

#### SHOT TYPE D — Top-Down Open Case
Camera position: directly above, looking straight down
- Position tripod over the open case (or hold phone directly
  above if tripod won't reach — use 2 second timer to avoid shake)
- Shows: interior rails and tray slots from above
- This angle demonstrates the modular tray system clearly

This shot is optional but strong for showing the interior
organisation system to customers who want to understand
how it works before buying.

Frames to take: 2

---

### PRODUCT 2: TOKEN TRAY ORGANISER (all colour variants)

#### SHOT TYPE A — Tray with Lid (styled, angled)
Camera position: 3/4 angle, slightly elevated
- Offset the tray and lid (lid partially underneath and behind,
  tray sitting slightly angled on top — as in your existing shots)
- Fill all tray compartments with dice and tokens
  Use a variety of colours — your existing multicolour dice
  selection works perfectly
- Leave 2-3 compartments empty to show compartment structure

Camera height: shoot slightly above the tray top edge
so you can see into the compartments

What makes this shot work:
- The colourful dice content immediately communicates
  the product's purpose
- The lid offset shows it's a lidded system, not just a tray

Frames to take: 3

---

#### SHOT TYPE B — Tray Empty (clean product shot)
Camera position: same 3/4 angle
- Remove all dice/tokens
- Clean tray thoroughly — any dust shows clearly in
  a lightbox on a bright colour
- This shot shows the compartment structure and print quality
  without visual clutter

Frames to take: 2

---

#### SHOT TYPE C — Tray Flat-Lay (from above)
Camera position: directly above
- Fill tray with dice/tokens as in Shot Type A
- Shoot straight down
- This is your most "shoppable" image — shows every
  compartment clearly

Frames to take: 2

## Between Variants — Checklist

Before switching from one accent colour to the next:
- [ ] Wipe case/tray with microfibre cloth (fingerprints)
- [ ] Confirm backdrop is still smooth and crease-free
- [ ] Do not move the tripod between Shot Types A and B —
      only adjust angle/height as specified above
- [ ] Check first frame of new variant on your phone screen
      before shooting the full set — confirm exposure looks
      right and colour is accurate

## Colour Accuracy Check

After your first 5-6 shots, zoom into the image on your
phone screen and check:
- Does the white background look white, or does it have
  a warm/cool colour cast?
- Does the accent colour look accurate to the real object?

If the background looks yellow/warm:
Your lightbox LEDs are warm-toned. Set white balance manually
in Pro mode — try "Fluorescent" or a custom Kelvin of 6000K.

If the background looks blue/cool:
Try "Cloudy" white balance preset or reduce Kelvin to 5000K.

Getting white balance right now saves correction time later.
A true neutral white background is essential for clean
background removal in post.

## After the Shoot — File Management

Immediately after shooting:
1. Back up all photos — AirDrop to laptop, Google Photos,
   or cable transfer. Do not risk losing a full shoot session
   to a phone issue.

2. Create a folder structure:
   northforge-photos/
   ├── skirmish-case/
   │   ├── black/
   │   ├── blue/
   │   ├── red/
   │   └── yellow/
   └── token-tray/
       ├── black/
       ├── blue/
       ├── red/
       └── yellow/

3. Name files clearly:
   skirmish-black-hero.jpg
   skirmish-black-front.jpg
   skirmish-black-open.jpg
   skirmish-blue-hero.jpg
   etc.

4. Do not edit or crop on your phone.
   Transfer full-resolution originals to your laptop first.
   Edit from originals, not phone-processed versions.

## Post-Shoot Processing (Same Day or Next Day)

Tools needed (all free):
- remove.bg — background removal
- Photopea (photopea.com) — edge cleanup if needed
- Squoosh (squoosh.app) — compression before Shopify upload
- Canva — final composition and any background swap

Processing order:
1. Select best frame from each set of 3
2. Remove background via remove.bg
3. Clean edges in Photopea if needed
4. Export as PNG transparent (for future compositing)
5. Export as JPG on white background (for Shopify listings)
6. Compress to under 500KB via Squoosh
7. Upload to Shopify — replace existing product images

Shopify image specs:
- Format: JPG for product images (smaller file, faster load)
- Dimensions: 2048x2048px maximum, 1200x1200px minimum
- Colour space: sRGB
- File size: under 500KB per image

## One Final Note

The most common mistake in lightbox product photography
is rushing through setups without checking the first frame.

Before shooting each new setup — stop, take one test shot,
zoom in on your phone screen, confirm:
- Sharp focus on the product
- White background is neutral white
- No visible creases in backdrop
- Product is centred and straight
- Accent colour looks accurate

Fix anything that needs fixing before shooting your full
set of frames. Two minutes of checking saves having to
reshoot an entire variant.
